Pluto

- 40 astronomical units away from Earth (5983914828 km

- Discovered in 1930

- Diameter of 2376.6 km

Fun Facts

1. Pluto is about 1,400 miles (2,380 km) wide. That's about half the width of the United States

2. Pluto orbits the Sun about 5.8 billion km away on average, about 40 times as far as Earth

3. A year on Pluto is 248 Earth years. A day on Pluto lasts 153 hours, or about 6 Earth days

Haumea

- 43 astronomical units away from Earth

- Discovered in 2004

- Average diameter of 100 km

Fun facts

1. Haumea takes 285 Earth years to make one trip around the Sun. As Haumea orbits the Sun, it completes one rotation every 4 hours, making it one of the fastest rotating large objects in our solar system.

2. Haumea has two known moons: Namaka is the inner moon, and Hi'iaka is the outer moon.
